TL;DR

A unified framework bridging behavior-cloning pre-training and reinforcement-learning fine-tuning.

Problem
BC Bottlenecks RL
In unseen contexts, the conditional p(a | c) collapses. BC assigns near-zero probability to optimal actions, leaving RL without a gradient signal.
Approach
Context Smoothing + Timestep Modulation
CSP noises the context to interpolate between p(a | c) and p(a), expanding exploration beyond the base policy and aliasing unseen contexts back in-distribution. TMRL dials σ per action chunk.
Result
Real-World VLA RL in < 1 hr
With a context-smoothed π0, TMRL reaches near-perfect success on three real-robot tasks: under an hour on WidowX (BridgeData-v2), under four on Franka (DROID).

The Need for Action Distribution Interpolation

On a 2D pointmaze from OGBench, we train two policies — one approximating the conditional p(a | c), the other the marginal p(a) — where c is the goal position. Both train on pointmaze-large and evaluate on the larger, out-of-distribution pointmaze-giant with an unseen goal (agent in yellow, goal in pink).

p(a | c): conditional (BC)
p(a): marginal (CSP, σ = T)

On the out-of-distribution maze, the conditional p(a | c) (left) collapses and pulls the agent further from the goal, while the marginal p(a) (right) covers broadly enough to include goal-reaching actions.

Takeaway. The conditional collapses on optimal actions; the marginal covers them only with low likelihood. This motivates a policy that interpolates between the two and a mechanism to learn where on that interpolation to operate.

Method

Fig. 3. (Left) CSP pre-trains pθ across all σ by corrupting context c with qσ, with σ as an explicit policy input. (Right) TMRL exposes σ as a coverage dial; πHL selects z and σ to maximize reward.

Pre-training: CSP
Context-Smoothed Policy
For each pair (c, a): sample σ, draw c ~ qσ(c | c), then optimize pθ(a | c, σ). Training across σ ∈ [0, T] yields a single weight set covering the full conditional → marginal spectrum.
Fine-tuning: TMRL
Timestep-Modulated RL
Freeze pθ. Run off-policy RL over πHL(z, σ | s). Large σ drives structured exploration beyond the conditional support; small σ recovers precise imitation. The choice is made per chunk, so modulation happens within a single rollout.

Validating context-smoothing. Context-smoothing broadens the policy’s context input via qσ; as σ rises, the resulting action distribution widens, smoothly interpolating from the conditional p(a | c) at σ = 0 toward the full marginal p(a) at σ = T.

Diffusion Noise on Pointcloud Contexts

On the dexterous-grasping suite, the context c is the object pointcloud. As σ rises, qσ progressively corrupts c with Gaussian noise: the pointcloud slowly loses its original structure, aliasing the out-of-distribution object with nearby in-distribution pointclouds seen during training. The policy can then borrow coherent grasps from related training contexts rather than fail on an unfamiliar shape.

σ Slider: The Conditional ↔ Marginal Spectrum

As σ rises, qσ(c | c) spreads mass over nearby contexts and the policy borrows coherent action chunks rather than dithering. The endpoints anchor the spectrum: σ = 0 recovers the conditional p(a | c), σ = T the marginal p(a). On unseen contexts the conditional collapses to a narrow action distribution that leaves RL with no reward signal; TMRL learns where on this spectrum to operate.

σ = 0 (conditional) σ = T/4 σ = T/2 σ = 3T/4 σ = T (marginal)
σ = 0.00 (conditional)

Action diversity grows with σ while coherence is preserved: the perturbation lives in context space, rather than action space.

Simulation Results

Action Coverage Before RL: Success@K

Success@K — the fraction of out-of-distribution states where at least one of K base-policy rollouts succeeds — directly measures action coverage (Eq. 3). On pointmaze-giant and cube-single, CSP exceeds BC and PostBC at every K, with the gap widest on cube-single where both baselines remain at zero.

CSP delivers coverage before RL begins. On cube-single, BC and PostBC remain near zero while CSP stays well above at every K: the action coverage RL needs to fine-tune efficiently.

We fine-tune a context-smoothed π0 on BridgeData-v2 (WidowX 250) and DROID (Franka). TMRL aliases the current context with related dataset behaviors whenever a broader marginal helps.

Learning curves. Base π0 can’t make progress because its action distribution is too narrow: no successful rollouts, no reward signal, no improvement. Context-smoothed pre-training naturally widens the policy’s action support, and TMRL leverages this diversity to reach near-perfect success on all three real-robot tasks.
