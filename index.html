TL;DR

We present a unified framework that bridges BC pre-training and RL fine-tuning.

01 — Pre-training
Context-Smoothed Pre-training (CSP)
We corrupt policy inputs (context c) with a forward-diffusion kernel qσ(c | c), training across all noise levels σ. This creates a single policy that smoothly interpolates between the precise imitation conditional p(a | c) and the broad, high-entropy marginal p(a) — a continuously tunable coverage dial.
02 — RL Fine-tuning
Timestep-Modulated RL (TMRL)
A high-level RL policy πHL jointly selects the latent action z and the noise level σ at each timestep. Large σ aliases the current context toward nearby training data for broad exploration; small σ recovers the precise imitation conditional for task completion — all within a single trajectory.

Overview

BC pre-training for RL fine-tuning is promising, but standard BC produces narrow, overconfident action distributions that collapse in unseen contexts—the policy assigns near-zero probability to potentially optimal actions.

Pre-training
Context-Smoothed Pre-training (CSP)
Corrupts policy inputs with a forward-diffusion kernel qσ(c | c) and trains across all noise levels σ. As σ grows, nearby contexts alias together and the induced distribution broadens continuously toward p(a), exposing a full coverage spectrum at a single set of weights.
Fine-tuning
Timestep-Modulated RL (TMRL)
Trains a high-level RL policy πHL that dynamically adjusts the diffusion timestep σ during fine-tuning — using large σ to alias contexts and approach the broad, high-entropy marginal p(a), and small σ to sharpen toward the precise imitation conditional p(a | c).

Unlike action-space perturbations (e.g. PostBC), CSP shifts the perturbation to the context, so the policy borrows coherent, structured action sequences from related training demonstrations rather than dithering with unstructured noise. CSP is input-agnostic: it applies identically to ground-truth states, 3D point clouds, and VLM embeddings in large VLA models. In real-world experiments, TMRL achieves near-perfect success on complex manipulation in under one hour while DSRL and other baselines remain near zero.

From Conditional to Marginal: Sweeping Context Noise σ

As σ increases, the corruption kernel qσ(c | c) spreads probability mass over nearby contexts — the policy borrows coherent action chunks from related training demonstrations rather than adding unstructured noise to actions. At σ = 0, behavior sharpens to the precise imitation conditional p(a | c); at σ = T, it approaches the broad marginal p(a). TMRL learns to exploit this spectrum during RL fine-tuning.

σ = 0 (conditional) σ = T/4 σ = T/2 σ = 3T/4 σ = T (marginal)
σ = 0.00 (conditional)

Behavior broadens continuously with σ. At low σ the policy faithfully imitates the demonstration for the current context; as σ rises, adjacent contexts alias together and the policy samples from a progressively broader mixture — coherent action structure is preserved throughout because the perturbation is in context space, not action space.

Why Interpolation Between p(a | c) and p(a) Is Necessary

We illustrate the core tension with a 2D pointmaze from OGBench. Two policies are trained on in-distribution data: one learning the conditional p(a | c) and one approximating the marginal p(a). Both are then evaluated on a larger, out-of-distribution maze with an unseen goal position. The conditional policy overcommits to the training distribution and steers the agent away from the goal; the marginal has the coverage to reach the goal region but varies too broadly for precise finishing. An ideal policy does both — broad marginal behavior far from the goal, precise conditional behavior near it.

Problem
p(a | c) — BC only
Overfit to training contexts. In novel states, the conditional collapses and assigns near-zero probability to optimal actions — RL cannot get a reward signal.
Insufficient
p(a) — marginal only
Broad coverage enables reaching the goal region, but too diffuse for reliable task completion. Optimal actions remain extremely unlikely.
Solution
CSP + TMRL
A single pre-trained policy that interpolates dynamically — using broad marginal coverage for exploration and sharp conditional precision for execution.
p(a | c) — conditional (BC)
p(a) — marginal (CSP, σ = T)

The fundamental tradeoff. The conditional (left) stays near-zero on the OOD goal; the marginal (right) has the coverage to approach it. CSP pre-trains a single policy capable of both extremes; TMRL learns when to use each — using broad marginal behavior early in the trajectory and sharpening to the conditional near the goal.

Method

Method

Fig. 3 from the paper. (Left) CSP pre-trains pθ across all noise levels by corrupting context c with the forward-diffusion kernel qσ(c | c). The noise level σ is provided as an explicit input so the policy can be queried at any conditioning strength at inference time. (Right) TMRL exposes σ as a coverage dial for the high-level RL policy πHL, which jointly selects the latent z and σ to maximize downstream reward.

A steerable base policy — trained at all noise levels via context smoothing — with a high-level RL agent that learns to modulate the diffusion timestep between the broad marginal for exploration and the precise conditional for execution.
Pre-training — CSP
Context-Smoothed Policy
For each training pair (c, a), samples a noise level σ and draws c ~ qσ(c | c), then optimizes pθ(a | c, σ). As σ grows, nearby contexts alias together and the induced distribution broadens toward p(a). Pre-training across all σ ∈ [0, T] exposes a full coverage spectrum at a single set of policy weights.
RL Fine-tuning — TMRL
Timestep-Modulated RL Policy
With the base policy frozen, πHL(z, σ | s) is trained by off-policy actor-critic RL to maximize cumulative reward. Large σ aliases the current context toward training data — providing structured, coherent exploration beyond the base policy's conditional support. Small σ recovers precise imitation for task completion. TMRL selects σ per step, enabling within-trajectory switching.
How TMRL Compares
Method Explores beyond p(a | c)? Coherent action structure? Adaptive per-step control?
RLPD △ Gaussian action noise only ✗ Dithering ✗
DSRL ✗ RL over policy's noise space — stuck at conditional support ✓ ✗
PostBC △ Single-step Gaussian action ensemble ✗ Dithering across timesteps ✗ Fixed at pre-training
TMRL (ours) ✓ Context aliasing — reaches beyond conditional support ✓ Borrows coherent action chunks from dataset ✓ RL-learned per step within trajectory

Empirical validation of context smoothing. A context-smoothed diffusion policy p(x,y | θ̂,σ) trained to produce 2D points on a unit circle conditioned on θ. As σ increases, the conditioning contours widen — the induced action distribution becomes broader and overlaps more across nearby contexts. At σ = 0, outputs concentrate precisely at θ = 0; at σ = T, the policy approaches the full marginal p(a).

Benchmark Results

Simulation Results

Action Coverage Before RL — Success@K

Success@K measures the fraction of out-of-distribution initial states where at least one of K base-policy rollouts succeeds — a direct empirical proxy for demonstrator action coverage (Eq. 3 in the paper). We evaluate on two OGBench tasks: pointmaze-giant (navigation with a larger state space than the training set) and cube-single (manipulation from initial positions beyond the training data). CSP achieves higher success@K at every K and across both tasks; the gap is most pronounced on cube-single, where BC and PostBC have essentially zero coverage.

CSP unlocks action coverage before RL. BC and PostBC plateau near zero on cube-single; CSP achieves meaningful coverage at all K. This coverage advantage directly translates to RL sample efficiency.

RL Sample Efficiency

The coverage advantage translates directly into faster and higher RL success. We evaluate against four baselines: RLPD (off-policy RL with Gaussian action noise), DSRL (diffusion policy steering over action noise space), PostBC (action-space Gaussian broadening), and SPiRL (hierarchical skill RL). TMRL consistently outperforms all baselines.

Real-World Experiments

Real-World RL with Context-Smoothed π0

< 1 hr
TMRL reaches near-perfect success on three real-robot manipulation tasks — sausage-in-pot and shrimp-in-drawer on WidowX 250, and press-button on Franka Panda — in under one hour of interaction. DSRL achieves near-zero success throughout, unable to reach beyond the base policy's conditional support.

CSP applied to VLA models. VLM embedding contexts are corrupted via qσ(c | c), giving π0 a tunable spectrum from precise imitation to broad marginal coverage. TMRL then trains πHL to select σ online during RL fine-tuning.

We post-train a context-smoothed π0 on BridgeData-v2 (WidowX 250) and DROID (Franka Panda) by noising VLM embedding contexts with the CSP objective. TMRL then modulates σ during fine-tuning—aliasing the current context with related dataset behaviors to approach the broad, high-entropy marginal when needed.

Real-world RL fine-tuning curves. Starting from a π0 that frequently fails, TMRL reaches near-perfect success on all three tasks in under one hour. DSRL shows negligible improvement — because it performs RL over the policy's diffusion noise space, it cannot reach beyond the base policy's collapsed conditional support in these novel task contexts.
