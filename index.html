Overview

We present a unified framework that bridges behavior cloning (BC) pre-training and RL fine-tuning.

  • Context-Smoothed Pre-training (CSP) injects forward-diffusion noise into policy inputs, inducing a continuous spectrum between precise conditional imitation and broader action coverage.
  • Timestep-Modulated Reinforcement Learning (TMRL) dynamically adjusts conditioning strength during RL fine-tuning, allowing the policy to interpolate between p(a | c) and p(a) for adaptive exploration.
Across diverse policy inputs (state, image, pointcloud, and VLM embeddings), CSP + TMRL significantly improves RL sample efficiency and enables real-world VLA fine-tuning in under one hour of interaction.

The Need for Action Distribution Interpolation

Standard BC policies learn a narrow conditional distribution p(a | c). In unseen or low-density contexts, this conditional support often collapses, assigning near-zero probability to potentially optimal actions. In a simple 2D point-maze, we show that p(a | c) is too narrow to reliably reach an unseen goal, while the marginal p(a) provides broader coverage but lacks precision near the goal. This motivates pre-training a policy that can interpolate between these distributions, then letting RL dynamically choose where to operate on that spectrum.

The need for action distribution interpolation. For an unseen goal position, p(a | c) remains narrow and under-covers useful actions, while p(a) offers broad support but excessive variation near task completion. An optimal strategy leans toward p(a) early for coverage and toward p(a | c) later for precision. CSP provides this interpolation mechanism and TMRL learns how to use it during fine-tuning.

Method

CSP trains a steerable policy by corrupting context inputs with a diffusion forward process and conditioning the policy on the noise level σ. This yields a single policy that can be queried from sharp conditional behavior (σ = 0) to broad marginal behavior (σ = T). TMRL then exposes σ as an explicit control variable during RL fine-tuning, enabling a high-level policy to adaptively trade off exploration and exploitation across states and even within a single trajectory.

Method overview. CSP pre-trains a policy across context noise levels, and TMRL fine-tunes a high-level controller that dynamically selects conditioning strength to balance exploration and precision.

Context-smoothing behavior across σ. As σ increases, context corruption induces structured aliasing across nearby contexts, broadening coverage while preserving coherent action chunks. At low σ, behavior is precise and strongly conditioned; at high σ, behavior approaches the marginal and emphasizes coverage.

Pre-training Results

We compare pre-training procedures by measuring action coverage on unseen tasks via Success@K. Relative to standard BC and PostBC, context-smoothed pre-training (CSP) improves zero-shot coverage across all evaluated out-of-distribution settings.

Context-smoothed pre-training improves action coverage before RL. Success@K is consistently higher with CSP than BC or PostBC, indicating broader and more reliable support over useful actions in out-of-distribution contexts.

Simulation Results

We evaluate TMRL on OGBench and LIBERO tasks against prior RL-with-priors baselines. TMRL reaches near-100% success on both OGBench tasks, outperforms strong baselines on final performance, and is the only method that reliably solves the longer-horizon LIBERO-90 setting where broader yet coherent exploration is required.

Simulation results. TMRL consistently improves RL sample efficiency and final success by adaptively modulating context smoothing. In hard OOD settings, this enables exploration beyond collapsed conditional support while remaining grounded in coherent behaviors from data.

Real-World RL with Context-Smoothed π0

We apply CSP to the π0 vision-language-action model by injecting forward-diffusion noise into its VLM embedding context during pre-training. This produces a context-smoothed π0 whose conditioning strength can be modulated at inference time — enabling TMRL to dynamically interpolate between broad coverage and precise execution during real-world fine-tuning.

Context-smoothing applied to π0. VLM embedding contexts are corrupted with a diffusion forward process parameterized by σ, producing a spectrum from sharp conditional behavior (σ = 0) to broad marginal behavior (σ = T). TMRL then learns to select σ dynamically during real-world RL fine-tuning.

Real-world results. The pre-trained base policy often reaches for the wrong object and fails to complete tasks reliably. TMRL improves to near-perfect success in under one hour of interaction by dynamically broadening and sharpening behavior as needed during fine-tuning.
