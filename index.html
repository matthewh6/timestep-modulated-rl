Overview

We present a unified framework for robot policy pre-training and RL fine-tuning that signifcantly improves fine-tuning sample efficiency RL.

  • Context-Smoothed Pre-training (CSP) widens action coverage during pre-training by adding diffusion noise to policy conditioning c. This enables interpolation between the conditional action distribution p(a | c) and the marginal p(a).
  • Timestep-Modulated Reinforcement Learning (TMRL) enables an RL policy to dynamically interpolate between p(a | c) and p(a) during fine-tuning by exposing a timestep control variable.
Together, they deliver strong RL sample efficiency across diverse policy inputs (state, image, pointcloud) and enable successful real-world VLA RL fine-tuning in under one hour.

The Need for Action Distribution Interpolation

Standard BC policies learn a conditional distribution p(a | c). When queried on unseen contexts, however, p(a | c) produces overconfident outputs that are often wrong—the distribution collapses in low-density regions, leaving little probability mass on potentially optimal actions. We propose pre-training a policy that can interpolate between the conditional action distribution p(a | c) and the marginal action distribution p(a). In a simple 2D point-maze, we show that the conditional policy is too narrow to reach an unseen goal, while the marginal policy provides broad coverage but lacks goal-directed precision. An optimal policy should blend both: relying more on p(a) early in a trajectory and on p(a | c) as the agent nears the goal. Context-smoothed pre-training enables TMRL to interpolate between these two extremes for more effective RL fine-tuning.

The need for action distribution interpolation. For an unseen goal position, the conditional action distribution p(a | c) remains narrow, lacking sufficient support for the agent to reach the goal. In contrast, the marginal p(a) provides the coverage necessary to explore toward the goal, though with too much variation once the agent is nearby. An optimal policy should track p(a) at the beginning of the trajectory and p(a | c) near the end. Context-smoothed pre-training enables TMRL to interpolate between both distributions for more effective RL fine-tuning.

Method

Context-Smoothed Pre-training (CSP) trains a policy by injecting forward-diffusion noise into its conditioning inputs. By conditioning the policy on both the noised context and the noise level σ, we obtain a single model that can be queried at any point on the spectrum from precise conditional imitation (σ = 0) to full marginal coverage (σ = T). During RL fine-tuning, TMRL exposes σ as an explicit control variable for a high-level RL policy, which learns to dynamically dial exploration up or down depending on the current state.

Context smoothing interpolates between conditional and marginal action distributions. As the noise level σ increases from 0 to T, the conditioning input transitions from a fixed point to a broad distribution. At σ = 0 the policy receives a precise context and produces tight, goal-directed trajectories; at σ = T the context is fully marginalised and trajectories fan out to cover the space. As σ → T the context is fully smoothed and the policy collapses to the marginal p(a) — broad exploratory coverage of the action space. Pre-training Results

We first compare the effect of various pre-training procedures on action coverage by measuring success rates on unseen tasks. We compare Context-Smoothed Pre-training (CSP) against standard BC and PostBC. CSP outperforms both baselines in zero-shot success rate across all out-of-distribution tasks evaluated.

Context-smoothed pre-training unlocks better action coverage before RL fine-tuning. We measure Success@K for CSP against standard BC and PostBC. CSP achieves greater success@K across all K on both tasks, demonstrating broader and more reliable action coverage from the pre-trained policy alone.

RL Fine-tuning Results

We evaluate TMRL on two tasks from the OGBench benchmark and two tasks from the LIBERO benchmark, comparing against prior approaches for RL with offline data. TMRL attains near 100% success rate in both OGBench tasks, outperforming the best baselines by 14% in pointmaze-giant and 200% in cube-single at final performance. In libero-goal, TMRL and RLPD both reach 100% success. However, for the longer-horizon libero-90 task, only TMRL explores sufficiently to achieve non-trivial success rates.

Simulation results. TMRL consistently outperforms prior methods. In cube-single, baseline methods achieve near-zero success due to insufficient exploration beyond the pre-trained policy's support; TMRL's context-smoothing enables coherent exploration that resolves this. In libero-90, only TMRL explores broadly enough to make progress on this long-horizon task.

We also demonstrate that TMRL extends to state-of-the-art vision-language-action (VLA) policies in the real world. Using a WidowX 250 6-DoF robot arm in the BridgeData-v2 setup, we fine-tune π0 on the BridgeData-v2 dataset with context-smoothing applied to the VLM embeddings, then perform TMRL fine-tuning on two held-out manipulation tasks.

Real-world results. While the pre-trained base policy fails on both tasks (often selecting the wrong object), TMRL fine-tunes to near-perfect success rates in under one hour of real-world interaction. DSRL, which cannot explore beyond the base policy's conditional distribution, shows negligible improvement.
