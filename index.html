Overview

BC pre-training for RL post-training is promising, but standard BC produces narrow, overconfident action distributions that collapse in unseen contexts—the policy assigns near-zero probability to potentially optimal actions.

Pre-training
Context-Smoothed Pre-training (CSP)
Injects forward-diffusion noise into policy inputs, creating a tunable spectrum from precise conditional imitation p(a | c) to broad marginal coverage p(a).
Post-training
Timestep-Modulated RL (TMRL)
Trains a high-level RL policy πHL that dynamically adjusts the diffusion timestep σ during post-training — using large σ to alias contexts and approach the broad, high-entropy marginal p(a), and small σ to sharpen toward the precise imitation conditional p(a | c).

CSP is input-agnostic: it applies identically to ground-truth states, 3D pointclouds, and VLM embeddings in large VLA models. In real-world experiments, TMRL achieves near-perfect success on complex manipulation in under one hour while many baselines remain near zero.

The Need for Action Distribution Interpolation

In an out-of-distribution 2D maze, the conditional distribution p(a | c) is overfit and leads the agent away from the goal. The marginal p(a) has coverage but too much variation for precise finishing. An optimal policy needs both—the broad, high-entropy marginal early, the precise imitation conditional late.

Solution
CSP + TMRL
A single policy that interpolates dynamically between the broad marginal and precise conditional when needed.

A useful policy tracks p(a) far from the goal and p(a | c) near it. CSP pre-trains a policy that can do both; TMRL learns when to switch.

Method

Fig. 3 from the paper. (Left) CSP pre-trains pθ across all noise levels by corrupting context c with qσ(c̃ | c). (Right) TMRL exposes σ as an explicit conditioning-strength dial for the high-level RL policy πHL.

A steerable base policy, trained at all noise levels, with an RL agent that learns to modulate the diffusion timestep between the broad, high-entropy marginal and the precise imitation conditional.

CSP samples a corruption level σ, draws a noised context c̃ ~ qσ(c̃ | c), and trains the policy on (c̃, σ). As σ grows, nearby contexts alias together and the distribution broadens continuously toward p(a).

TMRL then trains πHL to jointly select the latent z and the timestep σ at each step. Large σ aliases contexts toward the broad, high-entropy marginal p(a); small σ recovers the precise imitation conditional p(a | c)—even within a single trajectory.

Simulation Results

Action Coverage Before RL

Success@K measures the fraction of out-of-distribution initial states where at least one of K base-policy rollouts succeeds — a direct proxy for demonstrator action coverage. CSP outperforms standard BC and PostBC at every K, with the gap largest on cube-single where both baselines have near-zero coverage.

CSP unlocks action coverage before RL. BC and PostBC plateau near zero on cube-single; CSP achieves meaningful coverage at all K.

RL Sample Efficiency

This coverage advantage translates directly to RL. TMRL achieves near-100% success on both OGBench tasks and outperforms the best baseline by 101% overall. On long-horizon LIBERO-90, only TMRL achieves non-trivial success — DSRL's conditional distribution is too narrow to explore the task.

Real-World RL with Context-Smoothed π0

< 1 hr
TMRL reaches near-perfect success on two real WidowX manipulation tasks in under one hour of interaction, starting from a pre-trained π0 that often grasps the wrong object. DSRL achieves near-zero success throughout.

CSP for VLA models. VLM embedding contexts are corrupted via qσ(c̃ | c), giving π0 a tunable spectrum from precise to broad behavior. TMRL trains πHL to select σ online.

We post-train a context-smoothed π0 on BridgeData-v2 (WidowX 250) and DROID (Franka Panda) by noising VLM embedding contexts with the CSP objective. TMRL then modulates σ during post-training—aliasing the current context with related dataset behaviors to approach the broad, high-entropy marginal when needed.

Real-world results. From a base π0 that often fails, TMRL post-trains to high success in under one hour. DSRL shows negligible learning—it cannot reach beyond the base policy's conditional support.
